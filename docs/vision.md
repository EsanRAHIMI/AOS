# Vision

AOS is the kernel of a **role-aware AI Government platform**. Esan is the
primary owner and first operator, but the system must be designed so future
users, teams, government roles, organizations, and citizens can have their own
permission-scoped data and experiences on the same unified software kernel.

The current system is a real multi-service self-development kernel. The next
step is to connect it to real-world user contexts: calendar, email, files,
projects, income, expenses, opportunities, technology news, personal goals,
institutional goals, public-service workflows, and each user's preferred way of
working.

## North Star

Esan sets the initial direction and owns the platform. AOS turns approved
directions from authorized actors into plans, evidence, approvals, safe actions,
learning, and continuous improvement.

## Non-Negotiables

- No fake success: every capability is `real`, `fallback`, `manual_required`, or `not_configured`.
- Human control: sensitive, external, destructive, financial, identity, or public-impact actions require approval.
- Evidence first: reports and recommendations must cite system state, source data, or run output.
- Independent services: every service owns its lifecycle, env, port, domain, and manifest.
- Modern stable stack: TypeScript, strict schemas, HTTP contracts, managed persistence, audited events.
- User-scoped privacy: every personal, organizational, or citizen record must be isolated by tenant/user/role.
- Unified product evolution: software, schemas, agents, and contracts evolve globally and coherently.

## Future Shape

AOS should become:

- A personalized daily briefing and planning system.
- A business, organization, and public-service operating system.
- A software factory that safely evolves itself.
- A personal and institutional research/opportunity engine.
- A memory and decision layer for each authorized actor, with shared governance for the platform.
